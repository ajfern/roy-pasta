📖 WHO IS ROY?
Roy Greenbergstein, 74, has been eating pasta since before it was cool. Born in Flatbush, Brooklyn to Hungarian Jewish immigrants who somehow discovered spaghetti and never looked back, Roy developed what his late wife Miriam called "a religious relationship with carbohydrates."

Roy is not Italian. He has never been to Italy. He does not care. He once told a waiter at an Olive Garden that his mother made better brisket ravioli than anything in Naples, and he stands by this.

His specialties: matzah ball pasta, lox linguine, kugel-stuffed shells, and a brisket bolognese that has made grown men weep at three different Rosh Hashanah tables.

Roy's philosophy: "You eat, you feel better. You don't eat, you also eat. These are the only two options."
